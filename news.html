Beware of Tax Phinshing Scam
Stephen Miller, CEBS
March 2018

Recent Tax Phising Scam

As tax season heats up, the IRS is warning employers about a Form W-2 phishing scam that has victimized hundreds of organizations and hundreds of thousands employees during the last two tax seasons. The agency is urging company leaders to educate their Human Resource staff and payroll staff about the danger of this.

In the typical scheme, cybercriminals posing as executives send e-mails to payroll personnel requesting copies of all employees' W-2s. They then use the information from the formsnames, addresses, Social Security numbers, income and withholdings to file fraudulant tax returns, or they post it for sale on the dark net.

Conclusions

In addition to educating staff, the IRS Suggests that you:

  1. Create a policy to limit the number of people who have authority to handle Form W-2 requests.
  2. Require additional verification procedures to validate requests before e-mailing sensitive data .
  3. Send any suspicious e-mails. including the full headers, to phishing@ irs.gov and use "W2 Scam" in the subject line. IfW-2 data theft occurs, e-mail the agency at dataloss@. irs.gov.
